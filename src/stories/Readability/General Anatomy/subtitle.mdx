# Subtitle
The subtitle sits directly below the title and serves as a secondary layer of contextual information. Where the title names the subject or states the finding, the subtitle carries the details that qualify it: the geographic scope, the time period, the unit of measurement, the population covered, or a methodological note. Together, title and subtitle should make a chart fully self-contained without the reader needing to consult surrounding text.

At the FSO, the subtitle is particularly important in standalone outputs — printed reports, infographics, and dashboards — where charts are often extracted from their original context and reproduced elsewhere.

## Description
 
A subtitle is optional, but should be used whenever the title alone does not provide sufficient context to interpret the data correctly. It is not a second title: it should never restate what the title already says, and it should not introduce a second finding or a new topic.
 
The subtitle is the correct place for:
 
- **Units of measurement** — when a unit cannot fit naturally in an axis label (*"In thousands of full-time equivalents"*)
- **Time or geographic scope** — when not already encoded in the title or axes (*"Switzerland, 2010–2023"*)
- **Population or coverage notes** — when the data applies to a specific subset (*"Resident population aged 15 and over"*)
- **Index or base year references** — for indexed series (*"Index base: 2015 = 100"*)
It is not the correct place for source attribution (that belongs in the footer/caption) or for editorial commentary on the data.

# Introduction
## Four principles
The information or message communicated by the visualizations has to be understandable at first sight. Readability is therefore one of the fundamental principles of visualisation design.

It is also essential that the visualizations produced by the FSO follow a corporate identity and be quickly recognizable. This in order to quickly identify information that complies with the quality criteria of official statistics such as relevance, accuracy and reliability.
It is also important that static and interactive visualizations share a common design.

The FSO’s visualizations are designed for a large audience and must be accessible to the whole population. The guidelines are compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1.

The FSO disseminates statistical information by using visualizations across many communication channels (print publication, web, mobile, SOME). It is therefore very important that visualizations are reusable and cross-media.

## Guidelines audience
These visualization guidelines are mainly dedicated to visualization producers within the FSO and to partners of public statistics. It is of course also available to all who are interested in data visualization in dissemination.

## Compliance
These guidelines are in compliance with the W3C web standards, the AA level defined by the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1) for accessibility and the Confederation’s Corporate Design (CD-Bund).

## Description

A hierarchical table is used in order to showcase structured data on multiple levels. It is helpful to analyse the repartition or organisation of a phenomena in relation to its structure.
Lines are used to represent observations, organized in a hierarchical manner. Columns are assigned a variable each.
Hierarchical tables are not 100% machinereadable. Because hierarchy is created through indentation, or visual layout conventions, machines cannot reliably interpret structure, relationships or levels of indentation.

## Points of attention
- Not too many columns (3 to 5 columns maximum, this is in order to pay attention to mobile compatibility)
- Not too many hierarchical levels
- One subject per table
- No cell fusion to guarantee machine readability

## Description
A Choropleth Qualitative Map shows how different categories are distributed geographically (over an administrative unit). It is done through the spatial distribution of discrete categories represented by different colours (see colour guidelines). 
With nominal type of categories, the chosen colour types are qualitative, whereas with ordinal categories the chosen colours are sequential (colour gradient). Both can be present in a single map.

## Points of attention
- Keep the number of categories small
- Ensure adjacent regions are visually distinguishable
- Use a qualitative palette, distinct hues, equal perceptual weight

## Description
Coloured proportional symbols combine two visual encodings on a single map: symbol size represents an absolute quantity (such as a total count or aggregate value), while symbol colour encodes a second, independent variable. This second variable can typically be a qualitative category or a coarsely classed rate.

## Do and don't
- Provide two separate, clearly labelled legends
- Ensure size and colour encode genuinely distinct variables
- Use a solid stroke to keep symbols readable at all sizes
- Use colour for qualitative categories or classed rates
- Avoid too many categories
